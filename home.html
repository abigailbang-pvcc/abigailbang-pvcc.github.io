What is the U.S. Department of the Interior?

The U.S. Department of the Interior is an executive department of the United States federal government. It is a cabinet-level agency which is responsible for the management of cultural and natural resources withen the United States. Containing 11 bureaus, it oversees a variety of subjects from Wildlife, to land management, and Native American affairs.

Some of the many goals and priorities of the U.S. Department of the Interior include clean energy on public land and waters, imporving and maintaining positive relationships between government and native tribes, restoring and conserving land and water, equality and environmental justice, and advansing infrastructure.

Goals and Projects

- Increasing Clean Energy on Public Lands and in Public Waters -

With the project to increase clean energy solutions, The U.S. Department of the Interior is focused on greater solar, onshore and offshore wind, geothermal, wave and tidal energy projects.


- Maintaining the Relationship Between Native Tribes and the U.S Government -

By investing in Tribal land and indigenous communities, they will receive support, resources and infrastructure, while still having their self governance respected.


- 30% Conservation of Land and Water -

The America the beautiful initiative will aid in improving biodiversity, slowing extinction rates, discovering and promoting natural climate solutions with the intent of 30% conservation by 2030.


- Environmental justice -

Specific groups within the U.S. face greater levels of air pollution, toxic sites, and water pollution. The Justice 40 Initative seeks to adress this issue.


- Investing in America and Union Jobs -

The American Rescue Plan, Bipartisan Infrastructure Law, and Inflation Reduction Act help to address struggling habitats, the drought crisis, and strengthen wildland fire management, as well as address legacy pollution invest in tribal communities and create union jobs.

History

Founded in 1849, the U.S. Department of the Interior was created with the goal of managing domestic matters within the United States, containing many roles and responsibilities. Despite possessing a varied list of responsibilities, all the roles of the U.S. department of the Interior where related to development of the nation, or its people.

One of the most well-known elements of the U.S Department of the Interior is the National Park Service. Yellowstone National Park became the nation's first National Park in 1872, and in 1883 it became accessible by railroad. This development allowed more public access to the park, and quicker travel.

The National Park Service was not created until 1916, by President Woodrow Wilson, and Stephen T. Mather became its first director. The land known as Yellowstone National Park has a long history of human involvement, with 11,000 years of human history in the area. Many Native American tribes have been associated with Yellowstone, either living in the region, or traveling through it. The most known tribe affiliated with it is the Tukudika.

About Secretary Deb Haaland

The current Secretary of the U.S. Department of the Interior is is Deb Haaland, appointed by President Joe Biden on March 16th, 2021. Deb Haaland was the first Native American to serve as cabinet secretary, and she is a member of the Pueblo of Laguna.

Deb Haaland is the current Secretary of the U.S. Department of the Interior. She grew up in a military family, with her father in the marines, and her mother in the Navy. Secretary Haaland was the first Native American woman elected to lead a State Party, and she is one of the first Native American women to serve in Congress. In Congress, she focused on environmental justice, climate change, missing and murdered indigenous women.
