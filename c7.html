    Software Engineering

      Chapter 7: Software Engineering

      • Software Engineering - A type of computer science that uses principles for directing development of large, complicated software systems.
      • Integrated development environment (IDE) - Systems merging software development tools into a package.
      • Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) - An international organization focusing on computer science education, computer graphics, AI, and many other categories. It contains special interest grounds, called SIGs.
        • Purpose of ACM - It is responsible for the support of progress in arts, sciences, and information technology advancement.
        • Benefits of ACM membership - Full ACM digital library, member pricing for APCs, full-year online subscription, MemberNet newsletter, TechNews, Student Quick Takes, A free email forwarding address, Special discounts and access to valuable products, Participation in ACM Distinguished Speakers Program, CareerNews, ACM Queue, Ubiquity, Discounts on subscriptions, Optional ACM Skills Bundle Add-On, A full year (12 issues) print subscription, Voting for ACM Officers.
        • Student ACM membership - Full ACM digital library, member pricing for APCs, full-year online subscription, 4 issues of XRDS: Crossroads, MemberNet newsletter, TechNews, Student Quick Takes, A free email forwarding address, Special discounts and access to valuable products, Participation in ACM Distinguished Speakers Program, CareerNews, ACM Queue, Ubiquity, Discounts on subscriptions.
      • Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) - Consisting of electrical, electronics, and manufacturing engineers, it contains many technical societies. Some of these societies are the Computer Society, Aerospace and Electronic Systems Society, and Vehicular Technology Society.
        • Purpose of IEEE - One of its responsibilities is establishing standards.
        • Benefits of IEEE membership - Network with other technology professionals, Establish a professional profile highlighting your accomplishments, Join and participate in discussions on various technical interests, Create a group to share and collaborate on projects, Discover IEEE events and activities throughout the world.
        • Student IEEE membership - Students can: Join a community of 420,000+ technology and engineering professionals, interact, collaborate, and innovate, get resources and opportunities needed to keep on top of changes in technology, get involved in standards development, Network with other professionals, be mentored by professional engineers and technologists, obtain access to the largest library of electrical engineering, computer science, and electronics technical literature as well as the latest technology trends, industry news, and events, collaborate with IEEE colleagues and member groups, online or in person.
      • Software Life Cycle: Development phase
        • Requirements analysis
        • Design
        • Implementation
        • Testing
      • Modular Implementation - Software is split into manageable portions.
      • Unified Modeling Language (UML) - Developed to relate to object oriented paradigm, it is a more modern option for dataflow diagrams and data dictionaries.
      • Use case - It displays the proposed system as a large rectangle where interactions between users and the system can be seen as ovals. Users of the system are shown as stick figures.
      • Use case diagram - Captures a view of the potential system, which is displayed from a user's perspective.
      • Data relationships:
        • One-to-one - Each particular thing is associated with one other.
        • One-to-many - One thing is affiliated with multiple others.
        • Many-to-one - Muntiple things relate to just one.
        • Many-to-many - Multiple things are related to multiple others.
      • Design pattern - Predeveloped model used to solve issues that reoccur in software design.
      • Software quality assurance (SQA) - Groups tasked with ensuring quality control systems.
      • Software testing:
        • Glass box testing - Performed with awareness of the interior structure of the software by the tester
        • Black box testing - Tests that do not utilize prior knowledge of the interior composition
        • Beta testing - categorized as black box testing, it is when an early version of the software is provided to its future audience to determine its performance within real-life scenarios.
        • Alpha testing - Mimicing beta testing, it is performed at the site of the software developer
      • Documentation:
        • User documentation - Explains software features and their usage
        • System documentation - Description of the internal composition of the software. It relates to maintenance performed later.
        • Technical documentation - Description of proper installation, and correct servicing, problem reports, and updates.
      • Intellectual property law - Based on copyright principles, and patent law. The purpose is to permit the developer to realease a product to intended users, while retaining ownership.
        • Software license - Formal expression of developer rights in legal terms.
        • Software disclaimer - Disclaimers are frequently included in code by the developers, describing the limits of their liability.
        • Best defenses against software liability claims - Sound software developing principles are critical in development, and records should be produced providing proof to demonstrate applicable care was taken.
